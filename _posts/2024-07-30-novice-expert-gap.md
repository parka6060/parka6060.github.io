**Beginners find value in actionable guides, while "masters" find value in higher-level concepts.**

Actionable guides manifests itself in many forms.
1. Art tutorials that teach how to draw specific things
2. Coding guides for specific projects
3. Learning a piano song note-by-note

This is because step by step instructions give starters a sense of safety. The coding community refers to this as "beginners hell." In academic literature, this phenomenon is discussed under various terms, such as "tutorial purgatory", yes this is what it's actually called, or "the intermediate plateau." It's a state where guides offer enough instruction to complete specific projects, but learners feel lost and dependent when attempting to work independently. 

The dicodmey of this is there's huge value in instructions and guides, it allows beginners to achieve immediate results. The challenge lies in bridging the gap between following instructions and grasping underlying concepts.
